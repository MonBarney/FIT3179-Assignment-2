GDP per capita impact throughout the World

Gross Domestic Product (GDP) per capita is an important indicator in modern world. It shows the economic growth of any given country.

 

 

From the map, countries such as Australia, Greenland, Sweden and United States are doing much better than any other countries in the world. This is due to them in having high GDP per capita as compared to the other parts of the world.

 

 

In contrast, countries in Africa are not doing as well as the rest of the world as they have low GDP per capita . This could implies that they are underdeveloped.

From this interactive scatterplot, we can observed that there is a positive collearation between Population and Greenhouse Gases Emissions.

 

By using the slider provided, we are able to see the changes in the past 20 years. We are able to observe that the greenhouse gases emissions and population increases over the years.

 

Countries in Asia tends to have high population while emitting high amounts of greenhouse Gases whereas countries in Oceania have low population and emitting low amounts of greenhouse gases .

 

By selecting any continent in the legend provided, we are able to observe the change of each continents as each years passed. Asia move at a much quicker rate as compared to the rest of the continents.

This chart shows the amount of greenhouse gases emissions from 2002 to 2019.

We are able to observed that East Asia & Pacific region emits the most greenhouse gases. East Asia & Pacific regions which is the region that is near the wester Pacific Ocean, countries include countries such as China, Japan, Mongolia, North Korea, South Korea and Taiwan. If we refer back to the above scatterplot, we are able to observe that China produces the most greenhouse gases.

The Sub-Saharan Africa produces the least amount of greenhouse gases. Sub-Saharan Africa which lies at the south of Sahara, includes major countries like Algeria, DR Congo, Sudan and Libya. It is observed that the greenhouse gases produces by those countries are lesser than the one produced by other regions.

Overall, regions that produce less greenhouse gases emissions most likely have low GDP per capita , which also indicates that they are a underdeveloped region in the world.
